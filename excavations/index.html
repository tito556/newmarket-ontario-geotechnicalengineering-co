Underground excavation in Newmarket, Ontario, encompasses the full scope of works required to safely and efficiently create subterranean space within the complex glacial geology of the Oak Ridges Moraine. The local stratigraphy typically features a challenging sequence of interbedded Newmarket Till, a dense, overconsolidated diamict, along with sands, silts, and clays deposited by glacial Lake Algonquin. This geological variability demands a rigorous geotechnical investigation before any shoring design or cut is made. A proper investigation program is the non-negotiable foundation of any underground project, directly informing ground behavior predictions and the selection of support systems to comply with the Ontario Building Code and local municipal standards.

The methodology for characterizing ground conditions follows the Canadian Foundation Engineering Manual and applicable CSA standards, relying heavily on a combination of in-situ and laboratory testing. In-situ assessments often begin with a Cone Penetration Test (CPT) to provide a continuous profile of soil stratigraphy and pore pressure, which is critical for identifying water-bearing granular lenses that pose a significant risk to excavation stability. This data is complemented by a suite of In-Situ methods, including field vane shear tests in soft clays, and is verified by a targeted program of high-quality sampling for advanced laboratory analysis.

Typical projects in Newmarket range from deep building basements and utility trenching to trenchless installations beneath existing infrastructure on arterial roads like Yonge Street and Davis Drive. Each scenario presents distinct challenges, such as managing high lateral earth pressures in the till or controlling groundwater inflow in more permeable deposits. For projects involving controlled backfilling and compaction of structural fill around excavations, the field density test (sand cone method) is the standard method for quality assurance, ensuring that backfill materials meet the specified relative compaction to prevent future settlement. The interpretation of soil behavior for these projects is further refined through index testing, including grain size analysis (sieve + hydrometer) and Atterberg limits, which classify the fine-grained soils and their susceptibility to changes in moisture content.

The excavation process itself is a managed sequence of investigation, analysis, construction monitoring, and final verification. Deliverables include a comprehensive geotechnical interpretative report with clear shoring recommendations, groundwater control strategies, and specifications for structural fill. This data establishes a defensible earth pressure envelope for the design of foundations and temporary retaining systems. Our value proposition is the delivery of a fully integrated data set that reduces the risk of costly delays and ensures that your underground excavation in Newmarket proceeds with certainty, from the first test pit to the final lift of compacted backfill.
