The National Building Code of Canada (NBCC) and CSA A23.3 set clear expectations for foundation bearing and groundwater control, yet in Newmarket Ontario the real challenge lies underground. The Oak Ridges Moraine and the underlying glaciolacustrine sediments create a layered profile where silt, clay, and sand lenses alternate unpredictably, often within a single borehole. A standardized grouting approach simply does not work here. We design injection programs that respond to this stratigraphic complexity, targeting loose granular zones and weathered shale interfaces that routinely cause settlement and seepage problems on local sites. Before mobilizing a rig, we correlate grouting parameters with data from in-situ permeability testing to refine mix viscosity and set pressure limits, and we integrate findings from test pits to confirm overburden structure where access allows.

In Newmarket’s layered till, effective grouting is not about pumping volume; it is about matching the injection rheology to the precise gradation of the target horizon.

Process overview

Newmarket’s growth along the Holland River corridor and its expansion eastward onto the till plains have pushed construction into increasingly variable ground. Older industrial parcels near Davis Drive often conceal undocumented fill, while newer subdivisions encounter dense silt till that fractures under injection pressure rather than permeating uniformly. A grouting design that performs well in sandy outwash may fail completely in the stiff matrix-supported diamict just a few hundred metres away. Our methodology prioritizes staged injection with real-time pressure-volume monitoring, adapting the rheology from microfine cements to chemical solutions depending on the target formation’s grain-size distribution. For karst-prone limestone bedrock encountered below the drift, we incorporate pre-treatment verification through stone columns concepts adapted for void filling rather than soil reinforcement, ensuring the grout reaches cavities without uncontrolled migration into the overburden.

Local context

On several Newmarket projects, we have walked onto sites where the contractor had already attempted blanket compaction grouting only to see surface heave and zero improvement at depth. The culprit is almost always the same: treating the entire profile as homogeneous when the upper crust of desiccated clay is stiff enough to redirect injection upward. Without a layered design that segments the treatment into distinct horizons—each with its own refusal criteria—the grout takes the path of least resistance, missing the loose lenses that actually need consolidation. In excavations near the Fairy Lake area, we have also seen groundwater inflows increase after poorly controlled permeation grouting, because the injection pressure fractured the confining silt layer that was holding back an artesian aquifer. Our designs explicitly define maximum allowable injection pressures per stage using hydraulic fracture models calibrated to local in-situ stress conditions and verified with pre-grouting permeability profiles.

Top questions

How does the Oak Ridges Moraine geology affect grouting design in Newmarket?

The moraine deposits include interbedded sand, silt, and clay lenses that change over short distances. This variability forces a horizon-specific design approach: one injection parameter set will not work for the entire hole. We model each target layer separately and often combine low-viscosity permeation grout for sands with fracture grouting for the stiffer till units.

What verification methods do you specify for grouting in Newmarket?

We typically require a combination of pre- and post-grouting water pressure tests (Lugeon or packer tests), core recovery comparison, and in some cases cross-hole seismic tomography to confirm that the grout has filled the target voids or pore space without creating unintended pathways.

Can grouting design prevent groundwater inflow during excavation near the Holland River?

Yes, that is one of the main applications. We design curtain grouting schemes that create a low-permeability barrier around the excavation perimeter. The key is to seal the permeable layers without over-pressuring the confining silts; our designs include stage-by-stage pressure limits based on the overburden stress at each depth.

What is the typical cost range for a grouting design package in Newmarket Ontario?

A complete grouting design for a typical commercial or residential project in Newmarket, including site-specific parameter definition, injection grid layout, and verification testing specifications, generally runs between CA$1,750 and CA$5,000 depending on the number of treatment horizons and the complexity of the access constraints.

Do you design jet grouting for underpinning existing foundations in Newmarket?

We do. Jet grouting can be an effective solution for underpinning where access is restricted and soil mixing is required. Our designs define the jetting parameters—nozzle diameter, rotation speed, lifting rate, and grout pressure—based on the undrained shear strength of the native till, and we specify test columns before production work begins.
